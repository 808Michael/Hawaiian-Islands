Iolani Palace

Iolani Palace in Honolulu is a must-visit historical site, once the royal residence of the Kingdom of Hawaiis monarchs. It offers a glimpse into the opulent and fascinating royal history with guided and self-led audio tours available. The palace is revered as a sacred place, so visitors are asked to dress respectfully and photography is allowed without flash for personal use. Open from Tuesday to Saturday, its recommended to make reservations, although walk-ins are accepted based on availability. This is a great place to learn more about the history and culture of Hawaii.

Diamond Head

Diamond Head, known as LÄ“ahi to Native Hawaiians, is an iconic volcanic tuff cone and a must-visit destination in Honolulu, Hawaii. The summit hike offers breathtaking panoramic views of Honolulu, Waikiki, and the Pacific Ocean, taking you through historic tunnels and bunkers. Its a relatively easy hike, making it accessible for most fitness levels, and is one of the most rewarding experiences on Oahu. The crater emerged around 300,000 years ago and is part of the Honolulu Volcanic Series, with no chance of eruption today. As a protected state monument, it provides a unique opportunity to explore a significant geological and cultural site within an American state capital.

Hanamau Bay

Hanauma Bay is a stunning natural preserve located on the southeast coast of Oahu, Hawaii, known for its rich marine life and clear waters. Formed within a volcanic tuff ring, its a popular destination for snorkeling, showcasing over 400 species of fish and an abundance of green sea turtles. The bay has a history of overtourism, leading to measures like mandatory educational videos for visitors to protect its delicate ecosystem. Its also a Marine Life Conservation District, emphasizing the importance of respecting marine animals and coral heads. Despite challenges like coral bleaching, Hanauma Bay remains a treasured spot for both locals and tourists, offering a glimpse into Hawaiis underwater beauty.

Pearl Harbor

Pearl Harbor, a site of immense historical significance, offers a solemn and educational experience for visitors. You can explore the USS Arizona Memorial, which honors the lives lost during the 1941 attack. The Battleship Missouri Memorial provides a walk through history aboard the last battleship built by the U.S. Navy. The Pearl Harbor Aviation Museum and USS Bowfin Submarine Museum & Park offer insights into World War II aviation and submarine warfare. Plan your visit between 7:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., but remember the last shuttle to the USS Arizona departs at 3:00 p.m

Koko Head is a challenging and popular hiking trail on the island of Oahu, known for its steep ascent up an old railway track. The trail leads to the summit of Koko Crater, an extinct tuff cone, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. With 1,048 railway tie stairs, its a rigorous workout thats often compared to the Manitou Incline in Colorado. Despite the physical challenge, the hike is a favorite among locals and tourists for the rewarding vistas at the top, especially during sunrise or sunset. Its recommended to wear proper footwear, bring plenty of water, and start early to avoid the heat and crowds.
