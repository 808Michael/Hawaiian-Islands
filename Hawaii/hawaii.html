Mauna Kea

Mauna Kea is a dormant volcano and the worlds tallest mountain from base to summit. Its a sacred site with cultural significance and offers activities like stargazing at its observatories, hiking scenic trails, and exploring its rich heritage. The Maunakea Visitor Information Station provides resources for tourists. Its known for its breathtaking views and unique experiences above the clouds. At Mauna Kea, visitors can also immerse themselves in the rich cultural heritage of the mountain through educational programs and interactive exhibits at the Maunakea Visitor Information Station, offering a deeper appreciation for its significance to the Native Hawaiian community.

Waipio Valley

Waipio Valley is a stunningly beautiful and historically significant place. Its lush green walls rise dramatically from the valley floor, creating a breathtaking panorama. The valley is known for its black sand beach, towering waterfalls, and taro fields, offering visitors a glimpse into traditional Hawaiian agriculture. Historically, it was a significant site for Hawaiian culture, serving as a residence for Hawaiian alii (royalty) and a center for taro cultivation. Today, Waipio Valley is a popular destination for hiking, sightseeing, and connecting with the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Hawaii. Important Notice - Waipio Valley Road is closed to non-residents. Visitor access to the valley floor and the subsequent valleys is only possible as part of a tour. The Waipio lookout is still accessible for everyone.

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park is home to two of the world's most active volcanoes, KÄ«lauea and Mauna Loa, and spans from sea level to 13,680 feet. Established on August 1, 1916, it covers 323,431 acres and welcomes around 2 million visitors annually. The park is recognized as an International Biosphere Reserve and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can enjoy wilderness adventures, short walks, scenic drives, and learn about volcanic activity at the visitor centers. An entrance fee is required, costing $30 per vehicle or $15 per individual.

Akaka Falls State Park

Akaka Falls State Park is a stunning destination on Hawaii Island, featuring two breathtaking waterfalls within a short, family-friendly hike. The park's main attraction is Akaka Falls, a majestic 442-foot waterfall that plunges into an emerald pool, surrounded by a lush rainforest. Visitors can also view Kahuna Falls, a smaller but beautiful 100-foot waterfall. The 0.4-mile loop trail offers views of wild orchids, bamboo groves, and draping ferns. It's recommended to visit in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy the serene beauty of the falls.

Liliuokalani Gardens

Liliuokalani Gardens is a 24.14-acre park featuring Japanese gardens, located on Banyan Drive in Hilo, Hawaii. The park was donated by Queen Liliuokalani and is situated on the Waiakea Peninsula in Hilo Bay. It boasts Edo-style landscapes, Japanese architecture, and picturesque sights including rock wall-lined ponds and massive Banyan trees. The gardens serve as a serene gathering place for picnics, photography, and leisurely walks. It's known to be the largest such gardens outside of Japan, offering a tranquil escape with ocean views.
